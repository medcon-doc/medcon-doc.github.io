Collection Process - Non-Plain Text Data

This section describes in detail how the items were collected for those sources that were not available as plain text files. In these cases we relied on online subscriptions including daily downloads of issues, or in cases online descriptions were not possible direct purchases in the country of publication by an academic collaborator and subsequent shipment of the collected issues to Germany. The different venues of purchase including the strategies we used to fill gaps are presented in the table below.

Outlet Collection Process
BILD Online subscription of the national edition, daily manual download of the whole issue from epaper.bild.de in pdf format
→ missing issues: German National Library
Al-Akhbar Daily manual download of the national edition in pdf format from www.al-akhbar.com (single pages)
→ missing issues: direct contact to the editor in chief
Al-Hayat Daily manual download of the national edition in pdf format from www.alhayat.com (single pages)
→ missing issues: direct contact to the editor in chief
An-Nahar Daily manual download of the national edition in pdf format from www.annahar.com (single pages)
→ missing issues: direct contact to the editor in chief
Hürriyet No subscription of the Turkish edition in Germany possible. Therefore daily purchase of the Turkish edition by Prof. Dr. G. Karanfil at the İzmir Ekonomi Üniversitesi in Izmir.
→ missing issues: Hürriyet Avrupa, available at the library at the University of Mannheim
Sözcü Direct subscription of the printed edition
→ missing issues delivered to the printing company in Frankfurt were picked up by the researchers
